Welcome to Portfolio Builder

This tool allows you to visually build, preview, and export a professional portfolio website in minutes. No coding required.

1. Pick a Template

Start by navigating to Config > Templates in the sidebar. Choose a layout that fits your profession. Note: Loading a template will reset current data.

2. Customize Content

Go through the Sections in the sidebar (Hero, About, Projects, etc.). Update text, change fonts, pick colors, and upload your images.

3. Live Emulator Preview

Click the Toggle Preview button (or the floating Eye icon on mobile) to see exactly how your site looks on Desktop, Tablet, and Mobile.

4. Export & Publish

When you are done, click Export in the top header. You will receive publishing instructions and your final .html file.

How to Publish Your Website

!!!! Very Important !!!!

Step 1: Get Your File Ready

  • Inside your Portfolio Builder, click the Continue to Export button below.
  • A file named index.html will download to your computer.
  • Find that file on your computer and do not rename it, It should match exactly to this: index.html. (Websites need this specific name to work).

Step 2: Upload to GitHub

  • Go to GitHub.com and sign in.
  • Click the green New button on the left side to create a new repository (this is just a storage folder for your website).
  • Type a short name for it (like my-website).
  • Scroll down and check the box that says "Add a README file". (Do not skip this—it is a shortcut that lets you upload files directly!)
  • Click the green Create repository button at the bottom.
  • On the next screen, click the Add file button near the top right, then click Upload files.
  • Drag and drop your index.html file onto the screen.
  • Click the green Commit changes button.

Step 3: Make it Live on the Internet!

Choose either Choice A or Choice B. They both give you a free, live link to your site.

Choice A: GitHub Pages (Quickest)
  • On your GitHub page, click the Settings tab at the top.
  • Click on Pages on the left-hand menu.
  • Look for the "Branch" section. Click the dropdown that says "None" and change it to main.
  • Click Save.
  • Go grab a glass of water (it takes about 2-3 minutes). When you come back and refresh the page, your live website link will be sitting right at the top!
Choice B: Vercel (Faster Loading)
  • Go to Vercel.com and click Log in with GitHub.
  • Click Add New and choose Project.
  • You will see a list of your GitHub folders. Find the one you just made and click Import.
  • Click the blue Deploy button.
  • Wait about 10 seconds. You will see a congratulations screen with your live website link!
